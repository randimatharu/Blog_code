Subscript or Superscript

A subscript or superscript is a character (number, letter or symbol) that is (respectively) set slightly below or above the normal line of type. It is usually smaller than the rest of the text. Perhaps the most familiar example of superscriptssubscripts is in chemical formulas. For example, the molecular formula for glucose is C6H12O6.
